Neighbourhood Guide

Passeig de Gràcia & the Eixample

Your hotel — the Cotton House — sits on Passeig de Gràcia, Barcelona's most magnificent boulevard and the spine of the Eixample (the Extension). The Eixample was designed in 1859 by urban planner Ildefons Cerdà — a revolutionary grid of octagonal city blocks with chamfered corners (creating small squares at every intersection) and a vision of equal sunlight and green space for all residents.

The Eixample became the showcase of Catalan Modernisme — the Art Nouveau movement that produced Gaudí, Domènech i Montaner, and Puig i Cadafalch. Within 500 metres of your hotel: Casa Batlló (Gaudí, 1904–06), Casa Milà / La Pedrera (Gaudí, 1906–12), Casa Lleó Morera, and Casa Amatller — the Manzana de la Discordia (Block of Discord), where three rival Modernista architects built on the same block.

The Cotton House itself occupies a 19th-century neo-classical cotton merchants' guild — look at the original weaving machinery displayed in the lobby.

Friday, August 7 — Casa Batlló, Private Gothic Quarter Tour & Tapas Evening

Neighbourhood Guide

Gothic Quarter (Barri Gòtic) & El Born — Context for Tonight

The Gothic Quarter (Barri Gòtic) contains the remains of the Roman city of Barcino (1st century BC), including intact Roman walls visible inside the Cathedral cloister. The Cathedral of Santa Eulàlia was begun in 1298 — its famous cloister with 13 white geese (kept in honour of Santa Eulàlia, martyred at age 13) dates to the 14th century.

Plaça Reial — your meeting point tonight — was built in the 1840s on the site of a Capuchin monastery. The lamp posts were designed by a young Gaudí in 1878, one of his first public commissions.

El Born (officially La Ribera) is the medieval merchants' quarter east of the Gothic. The Basílica de Santa Maria del Mar (1329–1384) was built by the merchants and sailors of the Ribera — funded entirely by the community. Every citizen carried one stone from the beach to the construction site — a tradition called the bastaixos. The purest Gothic building in Barcelona, mathematically perfect proportions.
