Granada

July 30 – August 1 · Alternate options
Centro · Albaicín
Granada Tapas Tradition Unlike Madrid or Seville, Granada still offers FREE tapas with every drink ordered — a tradition that survives from when Alfonso X decreed wine must be served with food to prevent intoxication. Order a caña (beer) or glass of wine and a tapa arrives.

Sevilla Tapas Key Pringá (slow-cooked meat ragout on bread), salmorejo (chilled tomato cream), solomillo al whisky (pork in whisky-garlic sauce), espinacas con garbanzos (spinach with chickpeas — the Sevilla vegetarian signature), and cola de toro (bull-tail stew) are the five canonical Sevilla tapas. Stand at the bar (de pie) to save 10–25% over sitting. Sevilla is a paid-tapa city (€2–€8/plate).
