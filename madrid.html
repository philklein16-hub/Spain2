5:30 PM Afternoon Walk

El Retiro Park — Royal Garden, Crystal Palace & Fallen Angel

FreeRowboat €6/30 minUNESCO World Heritage Site

Created in the 1630s as a private royal garden for King Felipe IV — closed to the public for 200 years until 1868. Enter through the Puerta de Alcalá on Plaza de la Independencia, the neoclassical triumphal arch commissioned by Carlos III in 1778.

Key stops: The Estanque Grande (great lake) — rent a rowboat and look back at the city skyline. The Crystal Palace — built 1887 in iron and glass for the Philippine Islands Exhibition, modelled on London's Crystal Palace, now a free Reina Sofía art space. The Fallen Angel statue — one of the world's only public monuments to Lucifer, positioned at exactly 666 metres above sea level. The Paseo de las Estatuas — 18th-century kings originally made for the Royal Palace roof, rejected as too heavy. Exit south along the Paseo del Prado.

6:30 PM Tapas Crawl

La Latina Neighbourhood — Calle Cava Baja

€30–€45/couple tapas + drinks15-min walk from hotel

One of Madrid's oldest neighbourhoods, with roots in the 15th century. The name comes from the Hospital de La Latina, founded in 1499 by Beatriz Galindo — the scholar who tutored Queen Isabella I in Latin. Calle Cava Baja is the spine of the neighbourhood: a medieval street that once ran along the outer city wall (the cava — a ditch).

Start at Taberna Txakolina (Cava Baja 26) for Basque pintxos, then try Almendro 13 for classic Madrid tapas, El Tempranillo for wines. One or two stops only — leave room for Angelita at 8:30pm. Plaza de la Paja nearby is one of Madrid's most beautiful medieval squares.

Wednesday, July 29 — El Retiro Deep Dive, Barrio de las Letras & Reina Sofía

Cultural exploration day
9:00 AM

El Retiro Deep Dive — Rose Garden & Ahuehuete Tree

A second, slower visit while it's still cool. The Rosaleda — 4,000 rose bushes in geometric parterre beds, designed in 1915. The Parterre Francés near the Felipe IV gate — a formal French garden containing the Ahuehuete tree, a Mexican swamp cypress over 400 years old, one of the oldest trees in Madrid (Napoleon's soldiers are said to have hidden a cannon in its branches in 1808). The Palacio de Velázquez — a Moorish-influenced brick pavilion hosting free Reina Sofía exhibitions.

11:00 AM

Barrio de las Letras — Madrid's Golden Age Literary Quarter

Free walkSherry at La Venencia €3.50

Madrid's literary heart in the 16th–17th centuries — the Spanish Golden Age. Cervantes, Lope de Vega, Quevedo, and Calderón all lived within a few streets of each other. Cervantes lived and died on Calle Cervantes — plaque marks the spot. Lope de Vega lived around the corner on the same street.

Look down at the paving stones of Calle de las Huertas — lines of Spanish poetry embossed in brass. La Venencia on Calle Echegaray has been pouring sherry since 1929 — no photography, no cards, judgment if you order anything besides fino or manzanilla. The floor is covered in discarded napkins (tradition). One of the best bars in Spain.

The JW Marriott is central — Prado, Reina Sofía, Royal Palace, and Retiro are all walkable (10–20 min). Use taxis for longer trips (Malasaña, Huertas after dinner).

12 lines, 287+ stations. Runs 6am–1:30am (Fri–Sat until 2am). Nearest stations to JW Marriott: Banco de España (L2) or Retiro (L9).
