9:30 AM Walk

Barrio Santa Cruz & Plaza de España

Free2.5km · 90 min at leisure

Barrio Santa Cruz is the old Jewish quarter — the city had the largest Jewish population in medieval Spain. In 1391 a series of violent pogroms devastated the quarter; by 1492, under the Alhambra Decree, all remaining Jews were expelled. The quarter was remodelled for the 1929 Ibero-American Exposition into the picture-postcard labyrinth you walk today.

Key streets: Callejón del Agua — narrow alley along the Alcázar wall, where Washington Irving lived and wrote Tales of the Alhambra. Plaza de Doña Elvira — linked to the legend of Don Juan. Plaza de Alfaro — said to have inspired Romeo and Juliet's balcony scene. Patio de Banderas — Giralda framed by orange trees.

Then walk south to the spectacular Plaza de España (1929) — semi-circular monument to the Ibero-American Exposition, with ceramic tile alcoves representing each Spanish province, a canal, and rowing boats. Come early before the heat.

✦ Anniversary Experience · 12:00 PM

Private Olive Oil & Sherry Wine Tasting

€80–€120/coupledevourtours.com or hotel concierge

A private afternoon tasting of Andalusia's finest extra-virgin olive oils, local Manzanilla and Fino sherries, and artisan Manchego and Torta del Casar cheeses — led by a local sommelier in a traditional Sevillian bodega.

Seville sits at the heart of the world's greatest olive oil region (Jaén produces more olive oil than all of Italy) and the sherry-producing Jerez triangle is just 90km away. Cold, briny Manzanilla with aged cheese and peppery olive oil — one of the great flavour combinations of southern Spain.

Book through Devour Seville or Seville Food Tours — both offer private couple sessions. Or ask the Hotel Alfonso XIII concierge for a sommelier session in the hotel courtyard.

Triana — The "Independent Republic"

Free walkAcross the Triana BridgeCeramics ~€20

Triana sits across the Guadalquivir, linked to the old city by the Puente de Isabel II (1852). Trianeros consider themselves a separate people. Historically the city's working-class quarter: ceramicists, bullfighters, sailors, and gypsies lived here.

Azulejos (painted ceramic tiles) — the colourful geometric tiles defining Andalusian architecture — were produced in Triana's workshops for centuries. The tiles in the Real Alcázar, Plaza de España, and countless churches came from here. Buy hand-painted tiles directly from workshops on Calle Alfarería.

Triana is also one of the birthplaces of flamenco. Calle Betis runs along the riverbank with direct views back to the Seville skyline, the Torre del Oro, and the Cathedral — one of the finest riverside vistas in Spain.

Route: Cross Triana Bridge → Calle Betis (views) → Triana lanes → Calle San Jorge (ceramics) → Mercado de Triana → back across to Santa Cruz.

Getting Around Sevilla

Best · Walking
Why walk
Seville's entire tourist core — Cathedral, Alcázar, Santa Cruz, Alfonso XIII hotel — is within 10 minutes on foot. Triana is a 15-min walk across the Guadalquivir bridge.
Tram T1
Most useful for tourists. Plaza Nueva along Avenida de la Constitución past the Cathedral to Nervión. Single €1.40 — tap contactless bank card directly on the validator (simplest method).
Buses (TUSSAM)
Extensive network. Single €1.40, pay driver. C3 & C4 ring road, C5 inside centre. Tarjeta Turista: 1 day €5 / 3 days €10 — unlimited.
Taxis
White with yellow stripe. Santa Justa → Alfonso XIII: ~€10, 12 min. City to Triana: ~€7. Apps: Cabify, FreeNow, Uber.
To/from Santa Justa
Taxi: ~€10, 12 min — recommended with luggage. Aug 4 morning: leave by 7:15 AM for 08:37 train.
